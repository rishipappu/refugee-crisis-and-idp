The Central African Republic's

Refugee Crisis

& Internally Displaced Persons

The Central African Republic, or CAR, is a landlocked nation in central Africa, it borders the Democratic Republic of the Congo to the south and Chad to the north. It is a relatively young nation, having gained independence from France in 1960, 59 years ago. It’s a nation divided by faith, with significant tensions existing between the Christian majority and the sizeable Muslim minority. The country has never had a stable, democratic government. Ever since David Dacko took power in 1962, the country has been ruled by a succession of corrupt, military leaders. As a result, the Central African Republic is one of the world’s poorest countries, with a GDP per capita of only 418 dollars. There is little hope for the CAR to overcome its financial woes, despite large reserves of valuable natural resources such as: diamonds, exotic timber, gold, and uranium.
